# Website Not Showing on Google

## Overview

If your website does not appear in Google search results, the problem is often related to indexing or configuration errors.

Search engines must be able to crawl, interpret, and index your website before it can appear in results.

---

## Common Causes

### Robots.txt Blocking Search Engines

A robots.txt rule can unintentionally prevent search engines from crawling your site.

Related insight:

- [/insights/robots-txt-errors/](/insights/robots-txt-errors/)

---

### Noindex Meta Tags

Pages may contain tags that explicitly instruct search engines not to index them.

Related insight:

- [/insights/noindex-tag/](/insights/noindex-tag/)

---

### Sitemap Problems

Search engines rely on sitemaps to discover site structure.

Common problems:

- missing sitemap
- incorrect URLs
- outdated sitemap entries

Related insight:

- [/insights/sitemap-errors/](/insights/sitemap-errors/)

---

### DNS or Domain Configuration Errors

Improper DNS settings can prevent search engines from consistently accessing the site.

Related insight:

- [/insights/dns-errors/](/insights/dns-errors/)

---

## Quick Diagnosis Checklist

Check these items:

- Search `site:yourdomain.com` in Google
- confirm your sitemap exists
- check robots.txt rules
- verify pages are indexable

---

## Next Steps

If indexing problems exist, your website may remain invisible regardless of marketing or advertising efforts.
